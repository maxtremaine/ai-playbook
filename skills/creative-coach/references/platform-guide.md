# Platform Guide

Concrete paths for getting a creative project made, organized by medium. Each medium has a fidelity ladder; pick the rung the time budget buys. Time estimates assume focused work and basic familiarity with the tool. Do not shave them; double them only when there is a clear reason, like a first attempt in an unfamiliar tool or medium.

## Fidelity levels (shared vocabulary)

- **Rough**: exists and communicates the idea. Ugly is fine. For testing the concept on yourself or one trusted person.
- **Solid**: structurally complete, one revision pass, presentable to its real audience.
- **Polished**: multiple passes, details resolved, ready to publish or present without caveats.

Most projects should stop at solid. Polished is for work with a long shelf life or a high-stakes audience.

## Writing (essays, talks, posts)

| Fidelity | Budget    | Path                                                                                               |
|:-------- |:--------- |:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Rough    | 30-90 min | Talk the idea out in a Claude conversation, then dictate or type an ugly full draft.               |
| Solid    | 2-4 h     | Outline, draft, one structural pass, one line-level pass. Read the opening aloud; rewrite it last. |
| Polished | 4-10 h    | Solid, plus a cooling-off gap before the final pass and a read-aloud test of the whole piece.      |

Platform notes:
- Long-form argument: a blog or newsletter. The piece can breathe; the title does most of the distribution work.
- LinkedIn or social posts: the format is the constraint. Write to the visible-before-truncation window; front-load the hook.
- External Sherpa prose of any kind: apply outside-voice at drafting time, not as a cleanup pass.

## Presentation (talks, decks)

The deliverable at coaching fidelity is a talk track plus rough slide outlines: for each slide, the headline, the supporting text, and a description of any chart or visual. The built deck is a separate, later project.

| Fidelity | Budget | Path                                                                                              |
|:-------- |:------ |:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Rough    | 1-2 h  | Beat sheet of the argument plus a one-line headline per slide. Enough to test the arc on someone. |
| Solid    | 3-6 h  | Full talk track (spoken words, not bullet fragments) and a per-slide outline.                     |
| Polished | 6 h+   | Solid, plus the actual deck build and a timed run-through.                                        |

Platform notes:
- Talk track first, slides second, never simultaneously. Slides that precede the argument end up driving it.
- Describe charts in words before building them: what comparison it shows, what the viewer should conclude. If the sentence is weak, the chart will be too.
- Building the deck: Sherpa material goes through sherpa-brand-style; otherwise Keynote/PowerPoint for speed, Figma when the design matters.

## Visual (drawing, design, diagrams)

| Fidelity | Budget       | Path                                                                      |
|:-------- |:------------ |:------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Rough    | 30 min - 2 h | Paper sketch, Excalidraw, or a Claude-generated SVG/Mermaid diagrams.     |
| Solid    | 2-5 h        | Figma or FigJam for layouts and diagrams.                                 |
| Polished | 5 h+         | Figma with proper spacing/type discipline, or finished illustration work. |

Platform notes:
- Diagrams that explain structure: start with Claude SVG or Mermaid in-conversation; move to Figma/Excalidraw only if it needs to live somewhere else.
- Freehand drawing: Procreate (iPad) for illustration, paper-and-scan for speed. If skill is the bottleneck, time-box a 30-minute study before committing to the full piece.
- Branded Sherpa material: sherpa-brand-style governs colors, type, and layout. Do not freelance the look.
- AI image generation: good for mood boards and concepting, not for final branded assets.

## Interactive (code, prototypes)

| Fidelity | Budget       | Path                                                                             |
|:-------- |:------------ |:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Rough    | 30 min - 2 h | Single-file Claude artifact (HTML/React). No repo, no deploy, no dependencies.   |
| Solid    | 2-6 h        | Artifact matured until the interaction is real, then exported.                   |
| Polished | Days+        | A real repo built with Claude Code. Unless it will be maintained, stop at solid. |

Platform notes:
- The prototype's job is to make the interaction feel real, not to be the product. Fake the data, hardcode the states.
- If the idea is a workflow rather than an interface, a diagram may test it faster than code. Check before building.

## Audio / video

| Fidelity | Budget    | Path                                                                                                  |
|:-------- |:--------- |:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Rough    | Under 2 h | Loom or QuickTime screen recording, one take, no editing.                                             |
| Solid    | 4-12 h    | Descript for anything spoken (edits by transcript, fastest path for talking-head and screen content). |
| Polished | 12 h+     | Scripted, multi-take, real editing pass. Rarely worth it for a first version of anything.             |

Platform notes:
- Editing time runs 2-4x runtime for spoken content, more for anything with cuts. Budget from runtime backwards.
- Record clean rather than fix in edit: quiet room, decent mic, script or beat-sheet in front of you.
- If the content is an argument, consider whether it is actually a writing project that got dressed up as a video.

## Cross-medium tiebreakers

When two mediums both fit the idea and the budget:

1. Pick the one where the audience already spends time.
2. Pick the one the user can revise fastest, since first versions are wrong.
3. Pick the one that produces a reusable asset (a diagram embeds in a post, a talk, and a doc; a video embeds nowhere else easily).
