# Inbox Management

Triage my Gmail inbox. Process every unread email in the inbox that has not already been starred, then stop. Do not change any Gmail settings, filters, or labels.

## What counts as important

An email is **important** when a real human is asking *me* for something specific: a partner or customer with a direct question, an investor request, someone waiting on a decision or reply from me personally. Not marketing, not newsletters, not receipts or automated notifications, and not broad asks aimed at the Partnerships team rather than me. If an email looks suspicious (possible phishing, mismatched sender domain, unexpected payment request), it is not important; leave it alone.

Star each important email. Leave everything else unstarred and untouched.

## Draft replies for important emails

For each important email, create a draft reply (do not send) on the thread:

> Hey [first name],
>
> I have received your email and will come back to you in the next [N] days.
>
> Max

Choose N between 2 and 5 based on urgency and complexity: 2 for time-sensitive or simple asks, 5 for things that will take real work, 3 as the default. Do not add anything beyond the script. Skip the draft if I have already replied on the thread or a draft already exists.

## Rules

- Drafts only. Never send email on my behalf.
- Never mark emails as read, archive, delete, or move them.
- Skip emails that are already starred.
- If Gmail is unavailable, stop and report the error rather than retrying destructively.

## Report

When finished, summarize in a few lines: how many emails were reviewed, the sender and subject of each important email with the reply window chosen, and anything that looked suspicious with a one-line reason.
