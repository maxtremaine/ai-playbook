# Inbox Management

Triage my Gmail inbox. Process every unread email in the inbox that has not already been starred, then stop. Do not change any Gmail settings, filters, or labels.

## Categories and stars

Sort each email into exactly one category. Apply the matching star (my superstars are configured in this rotation order):

1. **Important → yellow-bang** (first star). A real human is asking *me* for something specific: a partner or customer with a direct question, an investor request, someone waiting on a decision or reply from me personally. Not marketing, not newsletters, not automated notifications, and not broad asks aimed at the Partnerships team rather than me.
2. **Receipts and confirmations → blue-info** (second star). Payment receipts, invoices, booking and order confirmations, calendar confirmations, shipping notices.
3. **Questionable → purple-question** (third star). Anything suspicious or unclear: possible phishing, mismatched sender domains, unexpected attachments or payment requests, or emails you cannot confidently place in another category.
4. **Everything else**: leave unstarred and untouched (newsletters, marketing, automated notifications, FYI threads where nothing is asked of me).

When in doubt between important and questionable, choose questionable. Never draft a reply to a questionable email.

## Draft replies for important emails

For each **important** email, create a draft reply (do not send) on the thread:

> Hey [first name],
>
> I have received your email and will come back to you in the next [N] days.
>
> Max

Choose N between 2 and 5 based on urgency and complexity: 2 for time-sensitive or simple asks, 5 for things that will take real work, 3 as the default. Do not add anything beyond the script. Skip the draft if I have already replied on the thread or a draft already exists.

## Rules

- Drafts only. Never send email on my behalf.
- Never mark emails as read, archive, delete, or move them.
- One star per email; skip emails that already have any star.
- If Gmail is unavailable, stop and report the error rather than retrying destructively.

## Report

When finished, summarize in a few lines: counts per category, the sender and subject of each important email with the reply window chosen, and anything flagged questionable with a one-line reason.
